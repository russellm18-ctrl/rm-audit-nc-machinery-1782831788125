// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Your equipment images—dozers, excavators, asphalt pavers, power systems—are almost certainly sitting on your server as unnamed, untagged binary files with zero JSON-LD schema connecting them to product type, location, or availability. There is no structured data signaling to Google that you are the Cat® dealer serving Anchorage or Spokane with rental excavators in stock today. Right now, a contractor searching "Cat excavator rental Anchorage Alaska" is landing on a competitor's page because your images are invisible to the crawlers that would have delivered that click.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: Your site verbally claims 24/7/365 emergency service and machine rebuilds with guaranteed pricing—two extremely high-stakes commitments to fleet managers and site superintendents who authorize five- and six-figure repair contracts. There is not a single image of a technician mid-rebuild, a parts warehouse showing that 96,000-part-number stock, or a documented job site rescue. A fleet coordinator evaluating whether to hand you a broken dozer at 3 a.m. needs visual evidence that your shop floor and crew match the claim; right now they have nothing but a sentence.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: Any Cat dealer competitor running geo-tagged, schema-embedded equipment photography tied to specific Washington and Alaska job sites—logging operations, DOT roadwork, remote off-grid power deployments—is capturing the exact project-context search traffic you are forfeiting. When a buyer searches "power systems rental off-grid Washington" and a competitor returns an image result showing a Cat generator on a remote Pacific Northwest job site with proper alt-text and location schema, that relationship starts without you in the room and your rental fleet revenue takes the direct hit.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Commission a structured visual asset capture across your Washington and Alaska locations that documents your parts inventory depth, active service bays, rental fleet condition, and power systems in real operational environments—every image delivered with enforced file naming conventions, embedded GPS/EXIF location data, descriptive alt-text tied to specific product lines and service territories, and JSON-LD markup connecting each asset to its corresponding equipment category and branch location. This is not a branding exercise—it is a data deployment that makes every image a crawlable, rankable, trust-building asset working 24 hours a day. Your 96,000-part-number stock claim and your guaranteed rebuild pricing are revenue-grade proof points that currently exist only as text; structured visual documentation turns them into conversion infrastructure.
